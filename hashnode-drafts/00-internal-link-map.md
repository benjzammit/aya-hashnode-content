## Recommended link density per article
Use:
- 2 to 4 internal links in the body where natural
- 1 short “Suggested internal links” section near the end if needed
- 1 AYA mention or link near the CTA if relevant

Avoid:
- linking the same target phrase multiple times in one article
- forcing links into every section
- heavy external linking unless it genuinely improves trust

## Reader journey model
The ideal path is:
1. discover a category or comparison article
2. click into the main definition page
3. move into trust/limitations content
4. move into a practical use-case article
5. reach an AYA CTA naturally

Example journeys:
- Synthetic Audiences vs Focus Groups → What Is a Synthetic Audience? → What Synthetic Audiences Can and Cannot Do → AYA CTA
- What Are Human Digital Twins in Research? → What Is AI-Native Research? → How to Use Synthetic Audiences for Concept Testing → AYA CTA
- How to Test Messaging Before You Spend on Campaigns → What Is a Synthetic Audience? → What Synthetic Audiences Can and Cannot Do → AYA CTA

## Priority anchor text themes
Use natural anchor text such as:
- synthetic audiences
- human digital twins
- AI-native research
- concept testing
- messaging testing
- what synthetic audiences can and cannot do
- synthetic audiences vs focus groups

Do not over-repeat exact-match anchors unnaturally.

## AYA linking rule
AYA should be linked when:
- the article moves from education into practical application
- the CTA is the most relevant place to mention the company
- the link helps the reader understand the service or next step

Do not force AYA links into every section.

## Ongoing rule
As more articles are added, each new article should:
- link to at least 1 pillar article
- link to at least 1 comparison or use-case article
- receive links from at least 1 related older article
